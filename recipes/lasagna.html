Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish. It's easy to make and always a hit. It's great for holiday parties, but you can make it any time of year. It's also a great way to use up leftover meat sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 1 (15 ounce) container ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef, onion and garlic over medium heat until meat is no longer pink; drain. Stir in the tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt and pepper. Simmer, uncovered, for 15 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  3. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook lasagna noodles for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg and 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese. Spread 1/2 cup of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Top with 3 noodles, 1/2 of the ricotta mixture, 1/2 of the mozzarella cheese and 1/2 of the remaining meat sauce. Repeat layers. Top with remaining noodles and meat sauce. Cover with aluminum foil.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 25 minutes. Sprinkle with remaining Parmesan cheese and let stand for 15 minutes before serving.
